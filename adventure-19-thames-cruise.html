Fun Facts About the River Thames!

As our cruise continued, we learned so many incredible things about this historic river!

  • Ancient River: The Thames is over 2,000 years old! Romans built the first London Bridge over it in 50 AD!
  • River Length: The Thames stretches 215 miles (346 km) from its source in the Cotswolds to the North Sea!
  • 33 Bridges: There are 33 bridges crossing the Thames in London, from Hammersmith to Tower Bridge and beyond!
  • Tower Bridge Opens: Tower Bridge can lift up in the middle! It still opens around 850 times a year to let tall ships pass!
  • Tidal River: The Thames is a tidal riverâ€”it actually flows backward twice a day as the tide comes in from the sea!
  • Wildlife Haven: Over 125 species of fish live in the Thames! Including seals who sometimes swim up from the sea!
  • The Great Freeze: In history, the Thames froze completely several times! Londoners held "Frost Fairs" on the ice with markets and games!
  • Thames Barrier: The Thames Barrier protects London from flooding! It's one of the largest moveable flood barriers in the world!
  • Royal Highway: Kings and queens traditionally arrive at state occasions by boat! The Royal Barge is still used for special ceremonies!
  • Clean River: The Thames is now one of the cleanest rivers flowing through a major city! It was once very polluted but has been restored to health!
