Amazing Oxford Street Facts!

Shopping Paradise

Oxford Street is 1.5 miles (2.4 km) long and has over 300 shops! It's one of the busiest shopping streets in Europe, with millions of visitors every year!

History

The street was originally called Tyburn Road and was a route to the infamous Tyburn gallows until 1783! It became Oxford Street and transformed into the shopping destination we know today!

Christmas Lights

Every year, Oxford Street is adorned with spectacular Christmas lights, a tradition started in 1959! It's one of London's most beautiful holiday sights!

Transport Hub

It is served by four major Underground stations (Marble Arch, Bond Street, Oxford Circus, Tottenham Court Road) and numerous bus routes! Easy to reach from anywhere in London!

Famous Landmarks

Key landmarks include Marble Arch at the west end and the iconic Selfridges department store! Oxford Circus, where it crosses Regent Street, is one of the busiest intersections in the world!

Culture & Events

Besides shopping, it hosts street performances, cultural events, and is a popular spot for people-watching! It's not just about shopping - it's about experiencing London life!
